PARAGRAPHS

A paragraph consists of one or more sentences that form a self-contained unit of discourse. The start of a paragraph is indicated by a new line.

Text is easier to understand when it is split up into a unit of text. For example, a book may have chapters. Chapters can have subheadings. Under each heading there will be one or more paragraphs.

Behoove
<dd>
verb (used with object), behooved, behooving.
  1. to be necessary or proper for, as for moral or ethical considerations; be incumbent on: It behooves the court to weigh evidence impartially.
  2. to be worthwhile to, as for personal profit or advantage: It would behoove you to be nicer to those who could help you.
    3. verb (used without object), behooved, behooving.
  3. Archaic. to be needful, proper, or due: Perseverance is a quality that behooves in a scholar.
